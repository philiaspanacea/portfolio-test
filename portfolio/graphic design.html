Graphic Design

"The Decided Light"

Overview

The title of this design “The Decided Light”, draws inspiration from Big Brother and 'the place where there is no darkness' from George Orwell's novel “1984” as well as the extravagance of Gatsby from F. Scott Fitzgerald's “The Great Gatsby.” Both books use symbolism to shed light on the moral darkness of the characters, and the use of light and color in my design reminded me of that. After a few sketch iterations, this piece was created with an achromatic palette in Adobe Illustrator, then with a chromatic palette in Adobe Photoshop. I took inspiration from the use of achromatic and chromatic value contrast and semi-abstraction in the work of artists Okuda and Brad Eastman.

Design

The Process

From the beginning of the process, I knew there was a clear meaning I wanted to convey, and I decided to achieve this meaning by hving a figure out of frame, with the viewer only being able to see one eye and its line of sigh being a large portion of the page.

I began looking for ways to incorporate more organic shapes and curvilinear lines into the design to create visual contrast between the pattern created by the triangles. I also made the line of sight somewhat transparent to add depth and make it clearer that it also acts as a 'spotlight' of sorts.

I begin to add color, starting with the line of sight since it's the focal point. In making the focal point transparent, I was able to play around with color theory, testing how different hues would change between whether they were under that layer or not. I also start adding contrasting hues and varying saturations to the triangle pattern.
